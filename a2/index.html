About Me

I am currently pursuing a Master's degree in Information Systems, with a background in Psychology. Over the years, I've grown passionate about bridging human-centered design with technical systems, exploring how data and technology can be applied to create meaningful and intuitive user experiences. I enjoy tackling complex challenges, finding clarity in them, and transforming ideas into tangible solutions.

Academic Background

I completed my undergraduate degree in Psychology, where I participated in several UX research and applied projects, such as developing digital tools for education and mental wellness. These experiences equipped me with strong research methods, data analysis skills, and an understanding of the psychological drivers behind user behavior. Now, as a graduate student, I am focusing on information systems development and integration, with an emphasis on combining user-centered design and data-driven approaches.

Professional Philosophy & Technical Skills

I believe technology only creates real value when it is closely aligned with human needs. Guided by this philosophy, my work and projects are built on the principle of human-centered design. My technical skills include user research, interaction design, and prototyping, alongside a working knowledge of front-end development and data processing. With this interdisciplinary background, I am able to connect user insights with technical development, making collaboration within diverse teams more effective.
